# Thinking, Fast and Slow by Daniel Kahneman

## 1. Long Detailed Summary for Blog

**Title:** How Your Brain Tricks You: *Thinking, Fast and Slow*

**Introduction**
Nobel Prize winner Daniel Kahneman explains the two systems that drive the way we think.
*   **System 1:** Fast, intuitive, emotional, automatic. (e.g., 2+2, Driving on an empty road).
*   **System 2:** Slow, deliberative, logical, effortful. (e.g., 17 x 24, Parking in a tight spot).

**The Problem**
System 1 is the hero and the villain. It saves us time, but it is prone to bias. System 2 is lazy and often just agrees with System 1.

**Key Biases**
1.  **Anchoring:** We rely too heavily on the first piece of information (the price tag).
2.  **Availability Heuristic:** We think things are more common if we can easily remember examples (e.g., fearing plane crashes more than car crashes).
3.  **Loss Aversion:** We hate losing \$100 more than we like winning \$100.
4.  **Sunk Cost Fallacy:** Throwing good money after bad.

**Conclusion**
We are not rational actors. We are irrational, but in predictable ways.

## 2. Long Form YouTube Video Script (10 Minutes)

**Title:** You Are Not Rational (The Two Systems of Thinking)

**[0:00-2:00] The Bat and Ball**
*   **Host:** "A bat and a ball cost \$1.10. The bat costs \$1.00 more than the ball. How much does the ball cost?"
*   **Host:** "Your brain screamed: 10 cents! But that's wrong. If the ball is 10c, the bat is \$1.10. Total \$1.20. The answer is 5 cents."
*   **Host:** "That was System 1 thinking. Fast. Intuitive. And wrong. Welcome to *Thinking, Fast and Slow*."

**[2:00-5:00] System 1 vs. System 2**
*   **Host:** "System 1 is your autopilot. It detects anger in a face. It answers 2+2. It is fast and cheap."
*   **Host:** "System 2 is your conscious mind. It does logic. It is slow and expensive (burns energy). The problem is, System 2 is lazy. It lets System 1 run the show."

**[5:00-8:00] The Biases**
*   **Host:** "Because System 1 runs the show, we fall for biases."
*   **Anchoring:** "If I show you a \$5000 watch, then a \$200 watch, the second one looks cheap. That's an anchor."
*   **Loss Aversion:** "The pain of losing is twice as powerful as the pleasure of gaining. That's why we hold onto bad stocks."

**[8:00-10:00] Conclusion**
*   **Host:** "You can't turn off System 1. But you can learn to recognize when it's tricking you. Slow down. Call in System 2."
