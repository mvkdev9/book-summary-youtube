# Rest: Why You Get More Done When You Work Less by Alex Soojung-Kim Pang

## 1. Long Detailed Summary for Blog

**Title:** The Science of Doing Nothing: *Rest*

**Introduction**
We think of rest as the absence of work. Alex Pang argues that rest is a skill. It is an active partner to work. The most creative people in history (Darwin, Dickens) worked only 4 hours a day.

**Key Insights**
1.  **Deliberate Rest:** Rest isn't just sleeping. It’s walking, hobbies, and deep play.
2.  **The 4-Hour Limit:** Cognitive work peaks at 4 hours. After that, returns diminish.
3.  **The Subconscious:** When you rest, your Default Mode Network activates. It solves problems while you aren't looking.

**Strategies**
*   **Morning Work:** Do the hard work early.
*   **Walk:** Darwin had a "thinking path." Walking stimulates creativity.
*   **Deep Play:** Have a hobby that requires focus (Painting, Climbing). It clears the mind better than TV.
*   **Sleep:** It’s not a luxury; it’s a cleaning cycle for the brain.

**Conclusion**
If you want to be great, learn to rest.
