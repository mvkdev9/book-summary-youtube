# The Six Pillars of Self-Esteem by Nathaniel Branden

## 1. Long Detailed Summary for Blog

**Title:** The Definitive Work on Self-Esteem: *The Six Pillars of Self-Esteem*

**Introduction**
Nathaniel Branden defines self-esteem not as a "feel-good" emotion, but as "the disposition to experience oneself as being competent to cope with the basic challenges of life and as being worthy of happiness." It is an internal reputation.

**The Six Pillars**
1.  **The Practice of Living Consciously:** Being present in what you do. Not living on autopilot.
2.  **The Practice of Self-Acceptance:** Being on your own side. Accepting your feelings and actions without judgment, even if you want to change them.
3.  **The Practice of Self-Responsibility:** Realizing that you are the author of your choices and actions. "No one is coming to save you."
4.  **The Practice of Self-Assertiveness:** Honoring your needs and values. Speaking your truth.
5.  **The Practice of Living Purposely:** Having goals and taking the actions necessary to achieve them.
6.  **The Practice of Personal Integrity:** Aligning your behavior with your values. Keeping promises to yourself.

**Conclusion**
Self-esteem is a practice, not a destination. It is something you *do* every day.
