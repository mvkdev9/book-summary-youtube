# The Psychology of Self-Esteem by Nathaniel Branden

## 1. Long Detailed Summary for Blog

**Title:** A New Concept of Man's Psychological Nature: *The Psychology of Self-Esteem*

**Introduction**
In this earlier work, Branden explores the theoretical and philosophical roots of self-esteem. He argues that self-esteem is a fundamental human need. Without it, we cannot function properly. It is the "immune system" of the mind.

**Key Insights**
1.  **Objective Value:** Self-esteem isn't just about feeling good; it’s about being "fit for life."
2.  **Rationality:** Man’s primary tool for survival is his mind. Therefore, self-esteem is tied to the use of one's mind. To be irrational is to destroy one's self-esteem.
3.  **Pseudo-Self-Esteem:** Many people fake self-esteem by seeking status, money, or approval. This is "Pseudo-Self-Esteem." It is fragile because it depends on others.
4.  **Emotions:** Emotions are the result of our value judgments. If you have low self-esteem, you will have irrational emotions.

**Conclusion**
The pursuit of self-esteem is the pursuit of rationality and survival.
