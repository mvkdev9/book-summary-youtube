# Waking the Tiger by Peter A. Levine

## 1. Long Detailed Summary for Blog

**Title:** Healing Trauma: The Innate Capacity to Transform Overwhelming Experiences: *Waking the Tiger*

**Introduction**
Peter Levine, a trauma therapist, noticed that wild animals are rarely traumatized, even though they face death every day. Humans, however, are easily traumatized. He argues that trauma is not a psychological disorder, but a biological one. It is "frozen" energy in the nervous system.

**Key Insights**
1.  **The Immobility Response:** When an animal can't fight or flee, it freezes (plays dead). In this state, the nervous system is at peak energy. If the animal survives, it "shakes off" the energy.
2.  **The Human Problem:** Humans often suppress the "shaking" or the natural discharge of energy because of social pressure or fear. This keeps the trauma energy trapped in the body.
3.  **Somatic Experiencing (SE):** Levine's method focuses on the physical sensations of trauma. Instead of re-living the story, you re-live the sensation and slowly release the energy.
4.  **Titration:** Releasing trauma energy in small, manageable "drops" rather than all at once.

**Conclusion**
The body has an innate wisdom to heal itself. We just have to allow the "Tiger" to wake up and finish its natural response.
