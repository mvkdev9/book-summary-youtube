# Hidden Potential by Adam Grant

## 1. Long Detailed Summary for Blog

**Title:** The Science of Achieving Greater Things: *Hidden Potential*

**Introduction**
We are obsessed with "Natural Talent." We look for the "prodigy" who is great from day one. Adam Grant argues that we should instead focus on **Potential**—the capacity to grow and improve. He believes that character skills, not cognitive skills, are the true predictors of success.

**Key Insights**
1.  **Character Skills over Cognitive Skills:** Discipline, Determination, and Proactivity are more important than high IQ. These skills allow you to grow even when the road is hard.
2.  **Become a Sponge:** The ability to absorb information and feedback from everywhere.
3.  **The Imperfectionist:** Perfectionism is a trap. It makes you afraid to take risks. You have to be okay with being a "klutz" at first to become a master later.
4.  **The Scaffold:** Building systems and structures (like mentors or habits) that support you until you can stand on your own.
5.  **Systems of Opportunity:** How we can redesign schools and workplaces to find the "Diamonds in the Rough."

**Conclusion**
Success is not about where you start; it's about how far you travel. You have more potential than you realize.
