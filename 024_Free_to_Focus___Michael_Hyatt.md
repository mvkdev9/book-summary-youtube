# Free to Focus by Michael Hyatt

## 1. Long Detailed Summary for Blog

**Title:** Achieve More by Doing Less: *Free to Focus*

**Introduction**
Michael Hyatt argues that we have productivity wrong. The goal isn't to get more done; it's to have more freedom. "True productivity is not about efficiency; it's about effectiveness."

**The Compass (Stop)**
1.  **Formulate:** Define what you want. What is your "True North"?
2.  **Evaluate:** What are you currently doing? Categorize tasks into the **Freedom Compass**:
    *   *Desire Zone:* High Passion, High Proficiency. (Do this).
    *   *Distraction Zone:* High Passion, Low Proficiency. (Fun but low value).
    *   *Drudgery Zone:* Low Passion, Low Proficiency. (Delegate this).
    *   *Disinterest Zone:* Low Passion, High Proficiency. (Boring but you're good at it. Careful here).
3.  **Rejuvenate:** Sleep, rest, and play are productivity tools.

**The Laser (Cut)**
1.  **Eliminate:** The best way to save time is to stop doing things.
2.  **Automate:** Use tools and templates.
3.  **Delegate:** If you can't eliminate or automate, give it to someone else.

**The Action (Act)**
1.  **Consolidate:** Batch similar tasks.
2.  **Designate:** Plan your week.
3.  **Activate:** Beat procrastination.

**Conclusion**
Focus on your Desire Zone. That is where you make your unique contribution.

## 3. Short Form Video Scripts (30-60 Seconds)

**Script 1: The Freedom Compass**
*   **Audio:** "Audit your life with the Freedom Compass. 1. Drudgery Zone: You hate it and you're bad at it. Delegate immediately. 2. Disinterest Zone: You're good at it, but you hate it. Delegate eventually. 3. Distraction Zone: You love it, but you're bad at it. Stop it. 4. Desire Zone: You love it and you're great at it. Stay here. This is where you win."

**Script 2: The Disinterest Trap**
*   **Audio:** "The most dangerous place to be is the 'Disinterest Zone.' This is stuff you are good at, but you don't enjoy. Like a CEO who is great at spreadsheets. Just because you *can* do it, doesn't mean you *should*. It drains your energy. Hiring someone to do what you are good at (but hate) is the ultimate level up."

**Script 3: Batching**
*   **Audio:** "Context switching is killing your brain. Stop checking email every 10 minutes. Use 'Batching.' Do all your emails in one hour. Do all your meetings on one day. Do all your creative work in one morning. When you stay in one mode, you flow. When you switch, you stall."

**Script 4: Eliminate, Automate, Delegate**
*   **Audio:** "The order matters. When you have a task, first try to ELIMINATE it. Does it need to be done at all? Second, AUTOMATE it. Can a computer do it? Third, DELEGATE it. Can someone else do it? Only if the answer is no to all three should you do it yourself. Protect your time."

**Script 5: Rest is Work**
*   **Audio:** "We think rest is the opposite of work. Michael Hyatt says 'Rest is part of the work.' You are a biological athlete. You cannot perform without recovery. Sleep, hobbies, and vacations are not 'time off'; they are 'refueling.' If you don't schedule maintenance, your machine will break."
