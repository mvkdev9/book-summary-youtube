# The Prophet by Kahlil Gibran

## 1. Long Detailed Summary for Blog

**Title:** A Timeless Guide to Life, Love, and Loss: *The Prophet*

**Introduction**
First published in 1923, Kahlil Gibran’s *The Prophet* is a collection of 26 poetic essays. The story follows Almustafa, a prophet who has lived in the city of Orphalese for 12 years and is about to board a ship home. Before he leaves, the people ask him for his wisdom on the fundamental aspects of human existence.

**Key Themes**
1.  **Love:** Love should not be a cage. "Love one another, but make not a bond of love." Let there be space between you so your souls can breathe.
2.  **Marriage:** Partners should be like the pillars of a temple—supporting the same roof but standing apart.
3.  **Work:** "Work is love made visible." If you cannot work with love, it is better to sit at the temple gate and take alms from those who do.
4.  **Giving:** You give but little when you give of your possessions. It is when you give of yourself that you truly give.
5.  **Joy and Sorrow:** They are inseparable. The deeper that sorrow carves into your being, the more joy you can contain.

**Conclusion**
*The Prophet* is a reminder that life's most common experiences are also its most sacred.

**Script 2: Work is Love**
*   **Audio:** "Do you hate your job? Kahlil Gibran has a warning for you. He says: 'Work is love made visible.' If you work with resentment, you are poisoning the world. **Script 3: Joy and Sorrow**
*   **Audio:** "Are you going through a hard time? Remember this: 'The deeper that sorrow carves into your being, the more joy you can contain.' Your pain is not a mistake; it is a deepening. **Script 4: On Giving**
*   **Audio:** "Most of us give when we're asked. But Gibran says: 'It is well to give when asked, but it is better to give unasked, through understanding.' True giving isn't about the money or the gift; it's about seeing a need and meeting it before the other person has to beg. When you give of yourself, you are truly living."

**Script 5: The Gift of Children**
*   **Audio:** "To the parents out there: Your children are not your children. They are the sons and daughters of Life's longing for itself. They come THROUGH you, but not FROM you.
