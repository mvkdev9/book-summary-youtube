# Bed of Procrustes by Nassim Nicholas Taleb

## 1. Long Detailed Summary for Blog

**Title:** Philosophical and Practical Aphorisms: *The Bed of Procrustes*

**Introduction**
In Greek mythology, Procrustes was a man who invited guests to sleep in his bed. If the guest was too tall, he would chop off their legs. If they were too short, he would stretch them. Taleb uses this as a metaphor for modern life: we try to fit reality into our flawed theories and systems, rather than changing our theories to fit reality.

**Key Aphorisms & Themes**
*   **Knowledge:** "The person you are most afraid to contradict is yourself."
*   **Work:** "They find it easier to spend money than to make it, and easier to make money than to give it away."
*   **The Modern World:** "The three most harmful addictions are heroin, carbohydrates, and a monthly salary."
*   **Success:** "Success is having your own 'bed' rather than trying to fit into one built by others."
*   **Wisdom:** "You are only truly wise when you stop trying to fix the people around you and start fixing yourself."

**Conclusion**
We are making ourselves miserable by trying to be "efficient" and "predictable." True life is found in the messy, the random, and the unexplainable.
