# Originals by Adam Grant

## 1. Long Detailed Summary for Blog

**Title:** How Non-Conformists Move the World: *Originals*

**Introduction**
Adam Grant explores how we can all become more original—not by being "geniuses" or "risk-takers," but by recognizing great ideas and managing our fears. He argues that "Originals" are people who take the initiative to make their visions a reality.

**Key Insights**
1.  **Risk Management:** Originals are not daredevils. They often balance extreme risk in one area (starting a business) with extreme caution in another (keeping their day job).
2.  **Quantity Leads to Quality:** To get a few great ideas, you have to generate a lot of bad ones. (e.g., Shakespeare wrote 37 plays, but only a few are masterpieces).
3.  **The Advantage of Procrastination:** Moderate procrastination can lead to more creative solutions because it gives the brain time to "percolate" the problem.
4.  **Strategic Optimism vs. Defensive Pessimism:** How to use anxiety as a motivator.
5.  **The Power of the Minority:** How to speak truth to power without getting fired.

**Conclusion**
Originality is a skill. By taking small risks and generating massive volume, we can all put a dent in the universe.
