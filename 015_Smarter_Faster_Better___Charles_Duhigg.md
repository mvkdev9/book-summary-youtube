# Smarter Faster Better by Charles Duhigg

## 1. Long Detailed Summary for Blog

**Title:** The Secrets of Being Productive in Life and Business: *Smarter Faster Better*

**Introduction**
Following *The Power of Habit*, Charles Duhigg investigates the science of productivity. He explores how we can improve our motivation, focus, and decision-making.

**Key Concepts**
1.  **Motivation (Locus of Control):** Motivation comes from feeling in control. To motivate yourself, make a choice. Even a small choice (like where to sit) triggers the feeling of autonomy.
2.  **Teams (Psychological Safety):** Google's Project Aristotle found that the best teams aren't the ones with the smartest people; they are the ones where everyone feels safe to speak up without fear of embarrassment.
3.  **Focus (Mental Models):** Productive people tell themselves stories about what to expect. They visualize the day. When reality hits, they aren't surprised; they are prepared.
4.  **Goal Setting (Stretch & SMART):** You need both.
    *   *Stretch Goal:* The big, audacious dream (e.g., Run a marathon).
    *   *SMART Goal:* The specific plan to get there (e.g., Run 3 miles on Tuesday).
5.  **Managing Others (Lean & Agile):** Push decision-making down to the person closest to the problem. Trust your team.

**Conclusion**
Productivity isn't about working harder; it's about making better choices about how we think.

## 3. Short Form Video Scripts (30-60 Seconds)

**Script 1: Locus of Control**
*   **Audio:** "Why are some people motivated and others lazy? It comes down to 'Locus of Control.' If you believe life happens *to* you (External Locus), you will be passive. If you believe you make things happen (Internal Locus), you will be driven. Prove to your brain that you are in charge. Make a choice. Even a small one. Take the wheel."

**Script 2: Psychological Safety**
*   **Audio:** "The secret to a high-performing team isn't talent; it’s safety. Google found that the number one predictor of team success is 'Psychological Safety.' Can I ask a dumb question without being judged? Can I admit a mistake? If the answer is yes, the team wins. If the answer is no, everyone hides their problems, and the ship sinks."

**Script 3: Mental Models**
*   **Audio:** "Focus isn't just staring at a screen; it's telling yourself a story. Top performers use 'Mental Models.' They visualize their day before it happens. They imagine the meeting, the conflict, the resolution. When the moment arrives, they don't have to think; they just execute the script. Be the narrator of your own life."

**Script 4: Stretch vs. SMART**
*   **Audio:** "You are setting goals wrong. You either dream too big and get overwhelmed, or dream too small and get bored. You need both. 1. A Stretch Goal: 'I want to run a marathon.' 2. A SMART Goal: 'I will run 3 miles this Tuesday at 6 AM.' The Stretch gives you the 'Why.' The SMART gives you the 'How.' Link them together."

**Script 5: The Marine Corps Bias**
*   **Audio:** "The Marines teach a 'Bias for Action.' When in doubt, do *something*. Stagnation is death. If you are stuck on a project, just make a move. Any move. Write a bad sentence. Make a messy sketch. Action generates information. You can steer a moving car, but you can't steer a parked one."
