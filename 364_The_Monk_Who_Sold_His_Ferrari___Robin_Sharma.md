# The Monk Who Sold His Ferrari by Robin Sharma

## 1. Long Detailed Summary for Blog

**Title:** The 7 Virtues of Enlightened Living: *The Monk Who Sold His Ferrari*

**Introduction**
This is a fable about Julian Mantle, a high-powered lawyer who has a heart attack and sells everything (including his Ferrari) to seek wisdom in the Himalayas. He returns to teach his former colleague the "Sivana System" for a life of purpose and joy.

**Core Concepts (The 7 Symbols):**

1.  **The Garden (The Mind):**
    *   Cultivate your mind. Don't let weeds (negative thoughts) take over. Stand guard at the gate of your mind.

2.  **The Lighthouse (Purpose):**
    *   You need a clear target (Dharma). The purpose of life is a life of purpose.

3.  **The Sumo Wrestler (Kaizen):**
    *   Constant and never-ending improvement. Self-mastery is the DNA of life mastery. Do the things you fear.

4.  **The Pink Wire Cable (Discipline):**
    *   Willpower is a muscle. Build it with small acts of discipline.

5.  **The Stopwatch (Time):**
    *   Time is your most precious commodity. Don't waste it. Live with urgency.

6.  **The Roses (Service):**
    *   "A little bit of fragrance always clings to the hand that gives you roses." Serve others.

7.  **The Path of Diamonds (Present Moment):**
    *   Happiness is the journey, not the destination. Enjoy the diamonds along the path.

**Conclusion**
Robin Sharma wraps deep wisdom in a simple story. It is a starter kit for self-improvement, covering mindfulness, goal-setting, and time management.
