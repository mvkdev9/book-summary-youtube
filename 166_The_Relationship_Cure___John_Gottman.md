# The Relationship Cure by John Gottman

## 1. Long Detailed Summary for Blog

**Title:** A 5 Step Guide to Strengthening Your Marriage, Family, and Friendships: *The Relationship Cure*

**Introduction**
John Gottman expands his research beyond marriage to all human relationships (friends, coworkers, children). The core concept is the **Bid for Connection**.

**Key Insights**
1.  **The Bid:** Any attempt from one person to another for attention, affirmation, affection, or any other positive connection.
2.  **Responses to Bids:**
    *   *Turning Toward:* Acknowledging and engaging.
    *   *Turning Away:* Ignoring (the most damaging).
    *   *Turning Against:* Attacking or being argumentative.
3.  **The Emotional Command Systems:** Seven systems in our brain that dictate our moods. Knowing your own and your partner's primary system helps you understand their bids.
4.  **The Role of Emotionally Intelligent Listening:** Listening for the "Bid" behind the words.

**Conclusion**
The quality of our lives depends on the quality of our bids. Small daily interactions are more important than big events.

## 2. Long Form YouTube Video Script (10 Minutes)

**Title:** The Secret to Better Friendships and Marriage (The Bid)

**[0:00-2:00] The Core Unit of Connection**
*   **Host:** "What is the smallest unit of a relationship? Is it a kiss? An argument? John Gottman says it’s 'The Bid.' Understanding this one concept can fix your marriage, your friendships, and even your relationship with your boss."

**[2:00-5:00] What is a Bid?**
*   **Host:** "A bid is any tiny attempt to get attention. If your child shows you a drawing, that’s a bid. If your husband says 'Traffic was bad today,' that’s a bid. If a coworker asks 'How was your weekend?', that’s a bid. We make hundreds of these every day."

**[5:00-7:30] How We Respond**
*   **Host:** "This is where the magic happens. You can **Turn Toward**—look at the drawing, agree about the traffic. You can **Turn Against**—'Stop bothering me!' Or you can **Turn Away**—say nothing and keep scrolling your phone. Gottman found that turning away is the most toxic. It makes the other person feel invisible."

**[7:30-9:00] Emotional Command Systems**
*   **Host:** "Why do some people want to play (The Jester) while others want to feel safe (The Sentry)? We all have different 'Command Systems.' Some of us crave adventure; others crave calm. If you know what your partner needs, you can make better bids."

**[9:00-10:00] Conclusion**
*   **Host:** "Look for the bids today. Turn toward as many as you can. It’s the easiest way to cure a relationship."

## 3. Short Form Video Scripts (30-60 Seconds)

**Script 1: The Bid for Connection**
*   **Audio:** "Your relationships aren't built on big vacations. They are built on tiny moments called 'Bids.' A bid is any attempt to get your attention. 'Look at that cloud.' 'I'm tired.' 'What's for dinner?' Every time you acknowledge a bid, you are making a deposit in the emotional bank account. Stop waiting for the big moments and start noticing the tiny ones."

**Script 2: Turning Away**
*   **Audio:** "What is the most hurtful thing you can do to someone you love? It’s not yelling. It’s ignoring them. John Gottman found that 'Turning Away'—not looking up from your phone when someone speaks—is the fastest way to kill a relationship. It makes people feel worthless. The next time someone makes a bid, look up. Be there."

**Script 3: Emotional Intelligence**
*   **Audio:** "The smartest people in the world are often the loneliest. Why? Because they lack Emotional Intelligence. They listen to the words, but they miss the bid. If your partner says 'The house is a mess,' they aren't just giving you a fact. They are asking for help and support. Listen for the underlying need, not just the data."

**Script 4: The 5 to 1 Ratio**
*   **Audio:** "How do you know if a relationship is healthy? Use the 5 to 1 ratio. For every one negative interaction (a fight or a criticism), you need 5 positive ones (a laugh, a hug, a compliment). If your ratio is lower than that, the relationship is in trouble. Start intentional giving today to balance the scales."

**Script 5: Stay Curious**
*   **Audio:** "The enemy of connection is 'Knowing.' You think you know everything about your best friend or your spouse. So you stop asking questions. You stop making bids. The Relationship Cure is Curiosity. Ask 'What are you thinking about right now?' or 'What is your biggest goal this year?' Don't assume. Explore."
