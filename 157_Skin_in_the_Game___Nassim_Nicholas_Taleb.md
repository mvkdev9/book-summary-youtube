# Skin in the Game by Nassim Nicholas Taleb

## 1. Long Detailed Summary for Blog

**Title:** Hidden Asymmetries in Daily Life: *Skin in the Game*

**Introduction**
Taleb’s central thesis is that for a society to function, people must share in the risks of their actions. If you have the "Upside" but not the "Downside," you are a parasite. If you make a mistake, you should be the one to pay for it.

**Key Insights**
1.  **Symmetry and Justice:** You should not benefit from others' losses. The "Silver Rule" is: "Do not treat others as you would not want them to treat you."
2.  **The Minority Rule:** An intolerant minority can impose its will on a tolerant majority. (e.g., If 10% of a population requires Kosher food, soon all food becomes Kosher because it's cheaper for the producer than running two lines).
3.  **Intellectual Yet Idiot (IYI):** Taleb’s term for "experts" who look at the world through theories but have no actual experience or consequences when they are wrong.
4.  **Lindy Effect:** For non-perishable things (ideas, books, recipes), their remaining life expectancy increases with every day they survive. If a book has been in print for 50 years, it will likely be in print for 50 more.
5.  **Agency Problem:** When someone (the agent) represents someone else (the principal) but their interests are not aligned. (e.g., A real estate agent wanting a quick sale vs. you wanting the best price).

**Conclusion**
Rationality is survival. And survival requires skin in the game.
