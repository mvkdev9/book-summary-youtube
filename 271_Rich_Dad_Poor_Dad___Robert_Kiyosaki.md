# Rich Dad Poor Dad by Robert Kiyosaki

## 1. Long Detailed Summary for Blog

**Title:** What the Rich Teach Their Kids About Money That the Poor and Middle Class Do Not: *Rich Dad Poor Dad*

**Introduction**
Robert Kiyosaki tells the story of growing up with two fathers: his real father (Poor Dad), who was highly educated but struggled financially, and his friend’s father (Rich Dad), who was an entrepreneur and one of the wealthiest men in Hawaii. He identifies the mindset shifts required to build wealth.

**Key Insights**
1.  **Assets vs. Liabilities:** The rich buy assets; the poor and middle class buy liabilities they *think* are assets. An asset is anything that puts money in your pocket (rentals, stocks). A liability is anything that takes money out (your home, your car).
2.  **The Rat Race:** Working a 9-5 to pay for a lifestyle that requires more working. To escape, you must build passive income.
3.  **Mind Your Own Business:** Work your day job, but spend your free time building your own business or portfolio.
4.  **Financial Literacy:** Schools teach you how to work for money; you must teach yourself how to make money work for you. (Accounting, Investing, Markets, Law).
5.  **Work to Learn, Not to Earn:** In your youth, choose jobs based on the skills you will gain (Sales, Marketing, Management) rather than the salary.

**Conclusion**
Wealth is a choice of education and mindset. Stop being a slave to a paycheck.
