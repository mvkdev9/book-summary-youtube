# Tiny Habits by BJ Fogg

## 1. Long Detailed Summary for Blog

**Title:** Small Changes that Change Everything: *Tiny Habits*

**Introduction**
BJ Fogg, a Stanford behavior scientist, introduces a method that contradicts most self-help advice. He argues that motivation is unreliable and willpower is a myth. To change behavior, you need to make it tiny.

**The Fogg Behavior Model: B=MAP**
Behavior (B) happens when three things come together at the same moment:
1.  **Motivation (M):** Your desire to do the behavior. (Highly variable).
2.  **Ability (A):** How easy it is to do. (The key lever).
3.  **Prompt (P):** The trigger. (No prompt, no behavior).

**The Tiny Habits Recipe**
The formula for a new habit is:
"After I [Anchor], I will [Tiny Behavior]."
*   **Anchor:** An existing routine (e.g., brushing teeth, pouring coffee).
*   **Tiny Behavior:** A super-small version of the new habit (e.g., do two push-ups, floss one tooth).
*   **Celebration:** Immediately feel good about it (e.g., say "Yes!" or do a fist pump). This wires the habit into your brain.

**Key Concepts**
*   **Motivation Wave:** Motivation fluctuates. Don't rely on it. Design habits for the "bad days" when motivation is low.
*   **Shine:** The feeling of success. You change best by feeling good, not by feeling bad. Celebration creates Shine.
*   **Pearl Habits:** Turning an annoyance (like a noisy neighbor) into a prompt for a good habit (relaxing your shoulders).

**Conclusion**
If you plant a tiny seed in the right spot, it will grow without coaxing. Tiny Habits are those seeds.

## 3. Short Form Video Scripts (30-60 Seconds)

**Script 1: Motivation vs. Ability**
*   **Audio:** "Stop waiting for motivation. It’s a trap. Motivation is like a wave—it goes up and down. You can’t build a life on it. Instead, focus on Ability. Make the task so easy that you can do it even when you have zero motivation. Don't aim for 30 minutes of meditation. Aim for 3 breaths. Make it tiny, and you make it consistent."

**Script 2: B=MAP**
*   **Audio:** "There is a formula for behavior: B=MAP. Behavior equals Motivation, Ability, and Prompt. If you aren't doing what you want to do, one of these is missing. Usually, the task is too hard (Ability) or you forgot (Prompt). Don't try to pump up Motivation. Just make the task easier and set a better reminder. Solve the equation."

**Script 3: Floss One Tooth**
*   **Audio:** "I want you to floss just one tooth. Sounds ridiculous, right? That’s the point. BJ Fogg calls this a 'Tiny Habit.' If you commit to flossing all your teeth, you’ll quit when you’re tired. If you commit to one, you can always do it. And once you start, you’ll usually finish the rest. Lower the bar to raise your success."

**Script 4: Celebration**
*   **Audio:** "Here is a secret to hacking your brain: Celebration. Habits aren't formed by repetition; they are formed by *emotion*. If you feel good after doing something, your brain says 'Let's do that again.' So, every time you complete your tiny habit, celebrate! Smile, clap, say 'Yes!' It feels silly, but it locks the habit in instantly."

**Script 5: Anchors**
*   **Audio:** "Post-it notes are terrible reminders. Use 'Anchors' instead. An Anchor is something you already do every day, like brushing your teeth or making coffee. Attach your new habit to that anchor. 'After I pour my coffee, I will open my journal.' The old habit triggers the new one. No willpower needed."
