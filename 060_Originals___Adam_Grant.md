# Originals by Adam Grant

## 1. Long Detailed Summary for Blog

**Title:** How Non-Conformists Move the World: *Originals*

**Introduction**
Adam Grant explores how people can identify original ideas and act on them without risking everything. Being "original" isn't about being a genius; it's about being willing to question the status quo.

**Key Insights**
1.  **Risk Mitigation:** Originals are not daredevils. They are actually risk-averse. They balance their risk in one area (starting a business) by being extremely cautious in another (keeping their day job).
2.  **Quantity Leads to Quality:** The most original thinkers are also the most prolific. (e.g., Picasso created thousands of works; only a few are masterpieces).
3.  **Strategic Procrastination:** Procrastinating can actually improve creativity. It gives the subconscious mind time to incubate the idea and find a new angle.
4.  **First-Mover Disadvantage:** Being the first to market is often a mistake. "Settlers" (who come in later and improve) have a better survival rate than "Pioneers."

**Strategies**
*   **Generate More Ideas:** The best way to have a good idea is to have many ideas.
*   **The "Sarick Effect":** When pitching, start with the downsides. It builds trust and makes you look smart.
*   **Find Your "Devil's Advocate":** You need someone to challenge your assumptions.

**Conclusion**
Originality is a choice. It is the act of rejecting the default and exploring whether a better option exists.

## 2. Long Form YouTube Video Script (10 Minutes)

**Title:** The Surprising Habits of Original People

**[0:00-2:00] The Warby Parker Story**
*   **Host:** "In 2009, four students had an idea to sell glasses online. Adam Grant had a chance to invest. He said no. Why? Because they were too slow. They all had backup jobs. They took months to build the site."
*   **Host:** "He thought they weren't 'Original' because they weren't taking big risks. He was wrong. Warby Parker is now worth billions. This is the first lesson of *Originals*."

**[2:00-5:00] Originals are Cautious**
*   **Host:** "We think entrepreneurs are gamblers. They aren't. Research shows that people who keep their day jobs while starting a business are 33% less likely to fail. They use the security of the job to take creative risks in the business. Don't quit your job yet."

**[5:00-7:30] Strategic Procrastination**
*   **Host:** "Procrastination is usually bad. But for creativity, it’s a tool. If you start a project and then walk away, your brain keeps working on it in the background. This is why Martin Luther King Jr. was still editing the 'I Have a Dream' speech minutes before he went on stage. He gave himself time to wait for the best idea."

**[7:30-9:00] Quantity over Quality**
*   **Host:** "Want a genius idea? Have 100 bad ones. Shakespeare wrote 37 plays, but only 5 are masterpieces. Edison had 1,000 patents, but most were useless. Original people just fail more often than the rest of us."

**[9:00-10:00] Conclusion**
*   **Host:** "Originality isn't a fixed trait. It’s a habit. Question the default."

## 3. Short Form Video Scripts (30-60 Seconds)

**Script 1: Keep Your Day Job**
*   **Audio:** "Thinking about quitting your job to start a business? Don't. Adam Grant found that 'Originals' are actually very risk-averse. Entrepreneurs who keep their day jobs are 33% more likely to succeed. Why? Because when you have financial security, you have the freedom to be truly creative. You don't have to make desperate decisions to pay the bills."

**Script 2: The Browser Test**
*   **Audio:** "What web browser do you use? If you use Chrome or Firefox, you might be more successful than people who use Safari or Internet Explorer. Why? Because Chrome and Firefox users had to *download* a different browser. They rejected the default. Original people are the ones who look at the 'standard' way of doing things and ask: Is there a better way?"

**Script 3: Procrastinate for Creativity**
*   **Audio:** "Procrastination isn't always laziness. Sometimes it’s incubation. When you start a task and then put it off, your brain stays 'open' to the problem. You start seeing connections in the world that you would have missed if you finished immediately. Don't rush the first draft. Let it simmer."

**Script 4: Quantity is Quality**
*   **Audio:** "The secret to being a genius is being a workhorse. Picasso created 50,000 pieces of art. Only a handful are famous. If you want to have a great idea, you have to have a lot of bad ideas. Originality is a volume game. Stop trying to be perfect and start being prolific."

**Script 5: The Sarick Effect**
*   **Audio:** "Next time you pitch an idea, start with the 3 biggest flaws. This is called the Sarick Effect. It sounds counterintuitive, but it works. It makes you look honest, it makes you look smart, and it takes the 'ammunition' away from your critics. Once you've stated the problems, you can spend the rest of the time solving them."
