# Hooked by Michael Moss

## 1. Long Detailed Summary for Blog

**Title:** Food, Free Will, and How the Food Giants Exploit Our Addictive Instincts: *Hooked*

**Introduction**
Following *Salt Sugar Fat*, Michael Moss digs deeper into the "Addiction" aspect of processed food. He argues that food companies have created a "toxic food environment" that exploits our primitive instincts. Processed food is as addictive as tobacco, but we treat it like a necessity.

**Key Insights**
1.  **The Speed of Hit:** The faster a substance hits your brain, the more addictive it is. Processed food (sugar and salt) hits the brain faster than tobacco or cocaine.
2.  **Memory and Emotion:** We associate processed foods with childhood memories and comfort. Companies use "Nostalgia Marketing" to trigger these deep emotional loops.
3.  **The "Pre-digested" Problem:** Processed food is designed to be absorbed instantly. This causes a massive dopamine spike that leads to a crash and a craving for more.
4.  **Biological Mismatch:** Our brains evolved to crave rare high-calorie foods. Now that they are everywhere, we can't turn off the craving.
5.  **The Ethics of Food:** Moss compares the food industry's tactics to the tobacco industry of the 1970s.

**Conclusion**
We are not just overeating; we are being "hooked" by a system that prioritizes speed and profit over biology.
