# The Consolations of Philosophy by Alain de Botton

## 1. Long Detailed Summary for Blog

**Title:** Wisdom for Everyday Problems: *The Consolations of Philosophy*

**Introduction**
Alain de Botton argues that philosophy should be practical. It should help us deal with the real problems of life: being unpopular, being poor, being frustrated, or being heartbroken. He matches six philosophers with six common human difficulties.

**The Six Consolations**
1.  **Consolation for Unpopularity (Socrates):** You don't need everyone to like you. You only need your own reason to be satisfied.
2.  **Consolation for Not Having Enough Money (Epicurus):** Friendship, freedom, and thought are more important than luxury.
3.  **Consolation for Frustration (Seneca):** Anger is the result of expecting the world to be perfect. Expect the worst, and you'll stay calm.
4.  **Consolation for Inadequacy (Montaigne):** We all feel weird and imperfect. Even the smartest person in the world has to go to the bathroom. Accept your humanity.
5.  **Consolation for a Broken Heart (Schopenhauer):** Love is a biological trick to make us reproduce. Don't take rejection personally; it’s just the "will to live" looking for a better match.
6.  **Consolation for Difficulties (Nietzsche):** Difficulty is necessary for greatness. No one achieves anything without suffering.

**Conclusion**
Philosophy is a "therapy for the soul." It provides the perspective needed to handle the "slings and arrows of outrageous fortune."
